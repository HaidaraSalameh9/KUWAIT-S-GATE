YOUR GUIDE TO PORTGATE

KUWAIT’S GATE TO GLOBAL MARITIME TRADE

Who Are We?

Kuwait Ports Authority aims to transport its ports into commercial gateways linking it to global economy with the latest, most efficient systems in line with the State of Kuwait’s vision for the 2035 Development plan for the maritime transport sector. Therefore, PortGate was created as an integrated service center overseen by Kuwait Ports Authority to clear maritime shipments of imports and exports coming thru Kuwait’s sea ports for individuals and corporations. This is achieved with the objective to enhance global economic ties by offering comprehensive administrative services linking both the governmental agencies and the private sector for speedy customs clearance, hence pushing for increased economic growth and the volume of regional trade for the State of Kuwait.

Our Mission

Establishing a pioneering integrated administrative system in Port Management in the MENA region.

Our Vision

Managing PortGate as a comprehensive administrative center for customs clearance and release of cargo utilizing international systems and standards to enhance local commerce to match its counterparts on a regional and global scale.

Our Services

PortGate offers all services pertaining to authenticating and completing all customs processes and procedures by offering customs clearance services and licensing services (imports licenses) and ratification (of invoices and country of origin certificates) signatories and governmental releases (approvals of restricted goods) in addition to issuance of the consolidated customs statement. portGate also offers maritime licensing and boat registration thru the ministry of communications services and also KPA services to stevedoring contractors and all other services related to the maritime commercial sector.

Who is…?

Customs Broker

In charge of completing all processes concerning customs clearance from all agencies and issuing the consolidated statement for the Department of customs in addition to settling all fees and the preparation of required requests in exchange for a fee that is agreed upon with the merchant/individual customer.

Maritime Shipping Agent

In charge of the shipping process and agreements with the international shipping vessels to deliver cargo and issuing shipping insurance and track shipments to their destinations. (tracking number and merchant/customer contact information)

Governmental Release Agencies

Each agency is specialized and has its own procedures and requirements depending on the type of cargo in each shipment in adherence to the codes and laws of the State of Kuwait. please see each agency’s requirements here

Individual Customer Requirements

  • Shipment info and country-of-origin certificate
  • Shipping policy and a representative of the local shipping agent
  • Civil ID/ Passport/ valid residency card
  • Consolidated customs statement (customs broker)
  • Customs fee
  • Each release governmental agency requirements
  • Temporary import license

Company Requirements

  • License and signatory
  • Shipment invoice and country-of-origin certificate
  • Insurance policy
  • Consolidated customs statement
  • Each release governmental agency requirements
  • List of items
  • Customs fees

Steps to receiving your shipment

Apply for an appointment through the website or app. When visiting PortGate, you will be directed by the reception staff and will guide you thru the necessary requirements for your shipment and the relevant agency to complete the process of clearing your shipment.

Upon contracting with the international shipping agent in importing or a local shipping agent for exporting, kindly ensure the issuance of a country-of-origin certificate. Also note that there may be a requirement to translate said certificate

Pay close attention to the tracking number and the contact information of the shipment owner for notification of the arrival of the shipment in order to complete the required procedures promptly and avoid any delays penalties and fees.

Upon the shipments’ arrival, the customer/company will be notifies via text message or email as listed in the shipping policy.

The customer/ company representative must visit PortGate to complete all necessary requirements and submit all requests and documents to agencies involved in the process.

If in cases the shipment requires lab testing or any other approvals from governmental authorities, once that is obtained the process of acquiring customs releases may continue and full fees are paid. Then proceed to the Department of Customs for inspection. An authorization of delivery will be issued to the merchant/ customer to receive their shipment from the port where it arrived. This can be done in person or through the customs broker for the delivery to a location of the customer choosing.
